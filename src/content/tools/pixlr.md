Pixlr is the most accessible AI-powered photo editor available online. Running entirely in your browser, it offers a surprisingly full-featured editing experience that rivals desktop software for everyday tasks. With three editions — Pixlr X (simplified), Pixlr E (advanced), and Pixlr Designer (for layouts) — it caters to everyone from casual users to serious designers. The interface will feel familiar to anyone who has used Photoshop, with layers, masks, blend modes, and a full tool palette.

The AI features punch above Pixlr's weight class. Background removal is fast and accurate, generative fill lets you extend images or replace objects naturally, and the AI expand tool can intelligently extend image boundaries. These tools are backed by a credit system that recharges monthly, so heavy users will want the Plus or Premium plans. The smart selection tools also deserve mention — they make masking complex subjects (hair, trees, transparent objects) much easier than manual methods.

Pixlr's main limitation is that it's browser-based, so performance degrades with very large files or complex projects. The free tier has ads and limits AI features, but it's still one of the most generous free offerings in the space. For students, casual creators, and anyone who needs capable photo editing without a subscription commitment, Pixlr is an excellent choice. Professionals with heavy workloads will still prefer desktop software.
