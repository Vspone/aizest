Databox is an AI-powered analytics platform designed for businesses drowning in data spread across too many tools. Instead of jumping between Google Analytics, HubSpot, Salesforce, Stripe, and dozens of other services to piece together what's happening, Databox centralizes everything into a single, unified view. The platform connects to 130+ native integrations out of the box, pulling metrics from marketing, sales, support, finance, and product tools into customizable dashboards that give teams one source of truth.

What sets Databox apart is **Genie**, its built-in AI analyst. Rather than digging through charts or writing complex queries, users can ask questions in plain language — *"How did revenue compare to last month?"* or *"Which campaign drove the most conversions?"* — and Genie surfaces the answer directly from live, verified data. Because Genie queries the actual metrics rather than generating text from scratch, it eliminates the hallucination problem that plagues most AI chat tools. It's like having a data analyst on staff who never sleeps and always works from the source of truth.

Beyond the AI layer, Databox excels at operational analytics. The 300+ pre-built dashboard templates let users go from zero to a polished executive view in minutes. Automated reporting sends scheduled snapshots to Slack, email, or mobile push, so stakeholders get updates without logging in. And the Goals feature ties OKRs directly to live data — teams can set targets and track progress in real time rather than waiting for end-of-month spreadsheets.

Databox's pricing is structured around data sources and AI usage rather than seats. The Free tier offers a solid entry point with three sources and a single dashboard. The Analyst plan at $59/month opens up unlimited dashboards and includes 500 AI credits for Genie queries. The Pro plan at $159/month offers unlimited users (ideal for agencies) but caps at only three data sources — a notable limitation. The full AI summary and benchmarking features are locked behind the Growth plan at $399/month, which can feel steep for smaller teams. Additional data sources beyond each tier's allotment also incur extra costs, so the total bill can climb as a business connects more tools. Still, for organizations that value a single pane of glass and plain-language access to their metrics, Databox delivers a compelling, well-polished experience.
