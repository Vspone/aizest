Make (formerly known as Integromat) is a powerful visual automation platform that lets you design, build, and automate workflows without writing code. Unlike traditional if-this-then-that automation tools, Make's scenario builder provides a full visual canvas where you map out complex, multi-step workflows with branching logic, loops, and data transformations. The platform connects over 1,500 apps and services, from Google Workspace and Slack to CRMs like Salesforce and data tools like Airtable, making it a versatile automation hub for teams and professionals.

## Visual Scenario Builder

At the heart of Make is the scenario builder — a drag-and-drop visual editor that shows your entire workflow as a connected diagram. Each scenario is built from modules representing different apps and actions, connected by arrows that define the data flow. You can add routers for conditional branching, iterators for processing lists of items, and aggregators for combining data. The real-time monitoring panel shows exactly what's happening at each step, making it significantly easier to debug complex workflows than with linear automation tools. Error handling is built in with automatic rollback — if a step fails, Make can retry, skip, or halt the scenario based on your configuration.

## Make vs. Zapier

Make and Zapier serve the same general purpose — connecting apps without code — but they approach automation differently. Zapier is simpler and faster to set up for straightforward automations (one trigger, one or two actions), with a massive ecosystem of 6,000+ apps. Make, on the other hand, excels at complex, multi-layered workflows. Its visual scenario builder gives you far more control over data transformation, error handling, and conditional logic. The trade-off is a steeper learning curve: Make's interface can feel overwhelming to newcomers compared to Zapier's streamlined setup wizards. However, for power users who need to build production-grade automations — ETL pipelines, multi-system sync operations, or data processing workflows — Make's flexibility is a significant advantage.

## Who Should Use Make

Make is best suited for professionals, teams, and students who have outgrown simple automation tools and need more control over their workflows. It's particularly strong in scenarios involving data transformation — pulling data from one source, reformatting it, and pushing it to multiple destinations with conditional routing. The generous free tier (1,000 operations per month) makes it accessible for testing and light use, while the paid tiers scale up to 80,000 operations per month for business-critical automation. If you're comfortable with logic and want an automation platform that grows with your complexity, Make is an excellent choice that offers outstanding value for what it can achieve.
